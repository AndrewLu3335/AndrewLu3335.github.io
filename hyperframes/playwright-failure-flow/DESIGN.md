# Playwright Failure Flow Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

A precise technical editorial motion graphic that extends the portfolio's white, near-black, and emerald visual system. The piece should feel like an engineering evidence review, not a futuristic AI commercial: large declarative typography, thin structural rules, bounded data panels, real report imagery, and deliberate page-turn motion. The tension comes from IBM Plex Sans for clear human explanation and IBM Plex Mono for machine evidence.

## Colors

- `#f7faf8` — tinted paper background
- `#171a18` — primary text and dark evidence panels
- `#087443` — automation and verified-evidence accent
- `#b42318` — failure state only
- `#cad4ce` — rules and secondary panel borders

## Typography

- IBM Plex Sans — statements, labels, and explanatory copy
- IBM Plex Mono — commands, classifications, and technical evidence

## Motion

- Medium-energy editorial push transitions between scenes
- Entrance timing establishes hierarchy: verdict, evidence, analysis, action
- Direction, duration, and easing vary within each scene
- No scene-content exit animation before transitions

## What NOT To Do

- No gradients, neon, purple, glowing AI brains, or generic futuristic imagery
- No claim that AI decides test pass/fail
- No claim that the generic report example came from Lace Up
- No credentials, private tokens, or account data
- No autoplay or audio dependency on the portfolio page
